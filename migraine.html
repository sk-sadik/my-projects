Migraine Care & Treatment

About Migraine

Migraine is a neurological condition that causes intense headaches, usually on one side of the head. It is often accompanied by symptoms like nausea, vomiting, and sensitivity to light and sound. Triggers include stress, lack of sleep, certain foods, and hormonal changes.

Rest & Recovery

Take proper rest in a quiet and dark room to reduce headache intensity. Avoid loud noises, bright lights, and stressful activities. Relaxation helps in faster recovery.

Hydration

Drink plenty of water to prevent dehydration, which can trigger migraines. Warm fluids and herbal teas may also help in relieving symptoms.

Healthy Diet

Eat regular and balanced meals. Avoid skipping meals as it may trigger headaches. Stay away from caffeine, chocolate, and processed foods if they worsen your symptoms.

Home Remedies

Applying a cold compress on the forehead can reduce pain. Practicing relaxation techniques like deep breathing and meditation may help. Adequate sleep is also important.

Medication

Pain relievers like paracetamol and ibuprofen can help. Specific migraine medicines such as triptans may be used under medical advice. Avoid overuse of medications.

Hygiene & Care

Maintain a regular sleep schedule and avoid stress. Keep track of triggers and try to avoid them. Stay in a calm and comfortable environment.

When to See a Doctor

Consult a doctor if migraines are frequent, severe, or worsen over time. Seek immediate help if there is sudden severe headache, vision problems, confusion, or difficulty in speaking.
