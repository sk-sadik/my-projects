Asthma Care & Treatment

About Asthma

Asthma is a chronic respiratory condition that affects the airways, making breathing difficult. It occurs when the airways become inflamed, narrow, and produce excess mucus. Common triggers include dust, smoke, pollen, cold air, and respiratory infections.

Rest & Recovery

Take proper rest and avoid physical exertion during breathing difficulty. Stay in a clean, dust-free environment and avoid exposure to smoke and allergens. Calm breathing helps reduce asthma symptoms.

Hydration

Drink plenty of warm fluids such as water, soups, and herbal teas. Hydration helps keep airways moist and reduces irritation in the respiratory system.

Healthy Diet

Eat a balanced diet rich in fruits and vegetables to support immunity. Avoid cold drinks, processed foods, and items that may trigger allergies.

Home Remedies

Steam inhalation may help in easing breathing. Warm fluids and ginger-based drinks can soothe the airways. Practice slow and controlled breathing techniques for relief.

Medication

Use inhalers and prescribed medications regularly as advised by a doctor. Do not skip doses and avoid self-medication. Quick-relief inhalers help during sudden asthma attacks.

Hygiene & Care

Keep surroundings clean and free from dust and allergens. Wash bedding regularly and avoid pets or triggers if allergic. Maintain personal hygiene to prevent infections.

When to See a Doctor

Seek immediate medical help if there is severe breathing difficulty, chest tightness, wheezing, or if inhalers do not provide relief. Frequent asthma attacks require proper medical evaluation.
