Skin Rashes & Allergy Care

About Skin Rashes & Allergy

Skin rashes and allergies occur when the skin reacts to irritants, allergens, infections, or environmental factors. Common causes include dust, pollen, food allergies, insect bites, chemicals, and poor hygiene. Symptoms include redness, itching, swelling, dryness, and irritation on the skin.

Rest & Recovery

Avoid scratching or rubbing the affected area as it can worsen the condition. Keep the skin relaxed and avoid exposure to heat, sweat, and irritants. Wearing loose and soft clothing helps reduce irritation.

Hydration

Drink plenty of water to keep the skin hydrated from within. Proper hydration helps in reducing dryness and supports skin healing.

Healthy Diet

Eat a balanced diet rich in fruits and vegetables to boost immunity. Avoid foods that may trigger allergies such as seafood, nuts, or processed foods if sensitive.

Home Remedies

Applying calamine lotion can soothe itching and irritation. Aloe vera gel helps in cooling and healing the skin. Cold compress can reduce swelling and redness.

Medication

Antihistamines like cetirizine can help reduce allergic reactions. Mild steroid creams like hydrocortisone may be used for inflammation. Avoid using strong medications without medical advice.

Hygiene & Care

Keep the affected area clean and dry. Avoid using harsh soaps or chemicals on the skin. Wash hands regularly and avoid contact with allergens.

When to See a Doctor

Consult a doctor if the rash spreads, becomes severe, or does not improve. Seek immediate help if there is swelling of the face, difficulty breathing, or signs of severe allergic reaction.
