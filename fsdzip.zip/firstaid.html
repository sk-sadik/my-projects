Accidental First Aid Care

About First Aid

First aid is the immediate care given to a person who has been injured in a minor accident. It helps prevent the condition from worsening and supports recovery until medical help is available. Common situations include cuts, burns, sprains, minor wounds, and bleeding.

Rest & Safety

Ensure the injured person is safe and away from danger. Avoid unnecessary movement, especially in case of sprains or injuries. Keep the person calm and comfortable.

Cleaning the Wound

Wash the affected area gently with clean water to remove dirt and bacteria. Use antiseptic solution to clean the wound and prevent infection.

Stopping Bleeding

Apply gentle pressure using a clean cloth or bandage to stop bleeding. Elevate the injured area if possible to reduce blood flow.

Home Care

Apply antibiotic ointment and cover the wound with a sterile bandage. For minor burns, use cool water and apply burn cream. For sprains, use ice packs to reduce swelling.

Medication

Pain relievers like paracetamol or ibuprofen can be used if needed. Use antiseptic creams or lotions to prevent infection. Avoid using unknown substances on wounds.

Hygiene & Care

Keep the wound clean and dry. Change bandages regularly and monitor for signs of infection such as redness or swelling. Wash hands before and after treating the wound.

When to See a Doctor

Seek medical help if the wound is deep, bleeding does not stop, there is severe pain, swelling, or signs of infection. Immediate care is needed for fractures, serious burns, or unconsciousness.
