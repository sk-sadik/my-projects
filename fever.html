Fever Care & Treatment

About Fever

Fever is a temporary increase in body temperature that usually occurs when the body is fighting an infection. It is not a disease but a symptom of underlying health conditions such as viral or bacterial infections.

Rest & Recovery

Taking proper rest is essential during fever. It helps the body conserve energy and fight infections more effectively. Ensure you get enough sleep and avoid physical exertion.

Hydration

Drink plenty of fluids such as water, coconut water, soups, and fresh juices. Staying hydrated helps regulate body temperature and prevents dehydration caused by sweating.

Healthy Diet

Eat light and easily digestible food like rice, fruits, and boiled vegetables. Avoid oily, spicy, and heavy foods as they can slow down digestion and recovery.

Temperature Control

Wear light clothing and keep the room cool. Use a lukewarm sponge or apply a wet cloth on the forehead to help reduce body temperature. Avoid extremely cold baths.

Medication

Paracetamol can be used to reduce fever and relieve discomfort. Always follow the recommended dosage and avoid self-medication. Do not take antibiotics unless prescribed by a doctor.

Hygiene & Care

Maintain personal hygiene by washing hands regularly and keeping your surroundings clean. Avoid sharing personal items to prevent the spread of infection.

When to See a Doctor

Seek medical attention if the fever is above 103°F (39.4°C), lasts more than 3 days, or is accompanied by severe symptoms such as breathing difficulty, persistent vomiting, chest pain, confusion, or seizures.

Medicines Required

Normal

Paracetamol
Ibuprofen
ORS (Oral Rehydration Solution)

High

Paracetamol (higher dose as prescribed)
Ibuprofen / Naproxen
Antibiotics (only if bacterial – doctor prescribed)

Severe

IV fluids (hospital)
Strong antibiotics (doctor only)
Antiviral drugs (if required)
